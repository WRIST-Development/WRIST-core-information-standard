## Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) documents the implementation guidance for the **Core Information Standard v2.0**, a comprehensive healthcare information model developed using WRIST (Wonderful Ramsey Systems Information Specification Tool). The standard defines a unified approach to capturing, storing, and exchanging person-centred healthcare information across UK health and social care settings. The Core Information Standard encompasses the complete spectrum of healthcare data, from basic demographics to complex care planning and end-of-life preferences. It is designed to support integrated care delivery by providing consistent data structures and terminologies that enable seamless information sharing between healthcare providers, social services, and other care organisations. ## Document Overview This DAK provides implementers with the necessary guidance to adopt the Core Information Standard v2.0, including: - Health interventions and evidence-based recommendations - Generic personas representing key user roles - User scenarios demonstrating typical workflows - Business process models with BPMN-style diagrams - Comprehensive data element definitions - Decision-support logic tables - Performance indicators and metrics - Functional and non-functional requirements - References to supporting standards and guidelines The standard aligns with UK healthcare terminology standards including SNOMED CT for clinical terms, NHS Data Dictionary codes, dm+d for medications, and UCUM for units of measure.
