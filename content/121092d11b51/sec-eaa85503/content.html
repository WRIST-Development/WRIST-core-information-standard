Member | Informal carers and family members supporting the person | Yes | Daily care support, advocacy, information provision, care coordination | **General Practitioner (GP)** - Primary care physicians use the standard to maintain comprehensive records, coordinate care across services, manage medications, and ensure continuity of care through transitions. **Community Nurse** - These professionals rely on the standard to access care plans, document home visits, monitor chronic conditions, and coordinate with GPs and specialists. **Social Worker** - Social workers utilise the standard to document assessments, manage safeguarding concerns, coordinate support packages, and ensure legal requirements are met. **Hospital Clinician** - Specialists access historical information, document investigations and procedures, plan discharges, and communicate with primary care teams. **Allied Health Professional** - Therapists use the standard to access functional assessments, document interventions, track progress, and coordinate equipment provision. **Care Coordinator** - These key professionals use the full breadth of the standard to coordinate complex care across multiple providers and ensure information flows effectively. **Care Home Manager** - Managers access resident preferences, care plans, risk assessments and legal information to ensure appropriate care delivery in residential settings. **Mental Health Professional** - These specialists document mental health assessments, risk factors, formulations and treatment plans while accessing broader health and social context. **Person/Patient/Service User** - Individuals can contribute their preferences, goals and 'About Me' information to ensure care is truly person-centred. **Carer/Family Member** - Informal carers access care plans and contribute valuable information about the person's needs, preferences and daily routines.
