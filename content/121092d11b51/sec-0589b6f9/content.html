## Introduction The Core Information Standard v2.0 defines the essential data elements for person-centered health and care records across the UK. This standard establishes a comprehensive framework for capturing demographic information, clinical data, social care needs, and personal preferences in a structured, interoperable format. ### Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides implementation guidance for the Core Information Standard v2.0, supporting healthcare organizations, social care providers, and technology vendors in adopting this foundational standard. The standard covers the complete spectrum of person-centered information from basic demographics through complex care planning, legal frameworks, and end-of-life preferences. ### Background Developed through extensive stakeholder consultation, the Core Information Standard v2.0 represents a significant evolution in integrated health and social care documentation. It emphasizes person-centered care by incorporating "About Me" sections alongside traditional clinical data, ensuring that individual preferences, communication needs, and personal circumstances are captured systematically. The standard aligns with UK regulatory requirements including the Equality Act 2010, Mental Capacity Act 2005, and supports initiatives like the Accessible Information Standard.
