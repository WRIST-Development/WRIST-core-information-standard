Introduction ## Purpose and Scope The Core Information Standard (version 2.0) defines a comprehensive framework for structuring and sharing person-centred health and care information across UK health and social care services. This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides implementation guidance for systems and services seeking to adopt this standard, ensuring interoperability and continuity of care. ## Background Developed using WRIST (Wonderful Ramsey Systems Information Specification Tool), this standard encompasses the full spectrum of person demographics, clinical information, care planning, and support services. It builds upon established NHS data dictionary standards and incorporates SNOMED CT, dm+d, and other recognised terminologies to ensure semantic consistency. ## Document Overview This DAK covers: - Generic personas and user scenarios for implementation - Business processes and workflows - Core data elements and their specifications - Decision-support logic and performance indicators - Technical and functional requirements The standard is designed to support diverse care settings from primary care through to specialist services, emphasising person-centred approaches and shared decision-making.
