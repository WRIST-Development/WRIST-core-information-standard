## Key Recommendations The Core Information Standard v2.0 supports evidence-based healthcare delivery through structured data capture and decision support. The following interventions are recommended for successful implementation: | Intervention | Recommendation | Source | Grade | |---|---|---|---| | Person-centred data collection | Implement comprehensive demographic and social context capture to support holistic care planning | PRSB Standards | A | | Standardised terminology | Use SNOMED CT, NHS DD codes, dm+d, and UCUM consistently across all data elements | NHS Digital | A | | Integrated care records | Establish shared care records linking health, social care, and voluntary sector information | NHS Long Term Plan | B | | Reasonable adjustments tracking | Systematically record and share accessibility requirements under Equality Act 2010 | Equality Act 2010 | A | | Medication reconciliation | Implement structured medication histories with dose directions and safety information | NICE Guidelines | A | | Advanced care planning | Capture and share end-of-life preferences including CPR decisions and preferred place of care | NHS England Guidance | B | | Risk assessment protocols | Use standardised risk assessment tools and share findings across care teams | CQC Standards | B | | Safeguarding procedures | Implement systematic safeguarding concern identification and escalation processes | Safeguarding Guidance | A | These recommendations are based on current UK healthcare policies, clinical guidelines, and regulatory requirements. Implementation should be tailored to local organisational contexts while maintaining compliance with national standards.
