## Health Interventions & Recommendations The Core Information Standard v2.0 enables comprehensive documentation of health interventions across multiple care contexts. Key recommendations focus on person-centered implementation and data quality. ### Key Recommendations | Intervention | Recommendation | Source | Grade | |---|---|---|---| | Reasonable Adjustments | Mandatory documentation per Equality Act 2010; use SNOMED CT coding (1108111000000107) | NHS England Accessible Information Standard | A | | Mental Capacity Assessment | Document capacity decisions with clear rationale; include best interests decisions where applicable | Mental Capacity Act 2005 | A | | Medication Management | Use dm+d coding for all medications; document administration details and discontinuation reasons | NHS Digital Dose Syntax Guidance | A | | Care Planning | Integrate person's goals, strengths, and preferences; document agreement with person/representative | NICE Guidelines on Person-Centered Care | B | | Risk Assessment | Document risk type, mitigation strategies, and review dates; include self-harm and safeguarding risks | NHS Safeguarding Guidelines | A | | Advance Care Planning | Record ADRT, LPA details, and preferred place of care/death with regular review | Gold Standards Framework | B | | Communication Support | Document communication methods, aids required, and language preferences | Accessible Information Standard | A | | Social Care Needs | Capture primary support reasons, care package details, and funding arrangements | Care Act 2014 Guidance | A | ### Context These recommendations emphasize the importance of capturing both clinical and social determinants of health. The standard's comprehensive approach ensures continuity of care across settings while maintaining focus on individual preferences and legal requirements.
