consent | ### CISV2.P1 - General Practitioner GPs serve as the primary point of contact for most healthcare needs, managing ongoing conditions while coordinating specialist referrals. They require comprehensive access to all information sections, particularly medications, problem lists, and care plans. ### CISV2.P2 - District Nurse District nurses deliver skilled nursing care in community settings, requiring detailed access to clinical procedures, wound care documentation, and medication administration records. They play a crucial role in identifying changes in condition and updating care plans. ### CISV2.P3 - Social Worker Social workers coordinate complex care packages and safeguarding responses. They need comprehensive access to social circumstances, legal information, and multi-agency plans while ensuring appropriate information governance. ### CISV2.P4 - Care Home Manager Care home managers oversee daily care delivery for residents with complex needs. They require access to all resident information to ensure safe, person-centred care while managing regulatory requirements. ### CISV2.P5 - Hospital Consultant Consultants provide specialist expertise for complex conditions. They need efficient access to relevant clinical history, investigation results, and current management plans to inform specialist interventions. ### CISV2.P6 - Mental Health Nurse Mental health nurses support individuals experiencing mental health challenges. They require access to risk assessments, mental health legislation status, and therapeutic interventions while maintaining therapeutic relationships. ### CISV2.P7 - Occupational Therapist Occupational therapists assess functional abilities and recommend adaptations. They need access to mobility assessments, equipment provisions, and environmental factors affecting independence. ### CISV2.P8 - Pharmacist Pharmacists ensure medication safety across care transitions. They require comprehensive medication histories, allergy information, and clinical context to optimise pharmaceutical care. ### CISV2.P9 - Paramedic Paramedics provide emergency care requiring rapid access to critical information. They need immediate visibility of advance decisions, key medical conditions, and current medications. ### CISV2.P10 - Patient/Service User Individuals receiving care are central to the information standard. They should have meaningful access to their information, contribute to care planning, and control information sharing preferences.
