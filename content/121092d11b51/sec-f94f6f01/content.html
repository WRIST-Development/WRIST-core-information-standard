## Purpose and Scope The Core Information Standard Version 2.0 defines the fundamental information requirements for person-centred health and care records across all settings in the United Kingdom. This standard ensures consistent capture and sharing of essential demographic, clinical, and social care information to support integrated care delivery. The standard applies to all health and care professionals, organisations, and systems involved in delivering or coordinating care. It encompasses acute, community, mental health, and social care settings, promoting seamless information exchange across organisational boundaries. ## Background Developed through extensive stakeholder consultation, this standard addresses the critical need for standardised information capture in an increasingly integrated care landscape. It builds upon previous PRSB standards while incorporating lessons learned from implementation experiences and evolving care models. The standard supports the NHS Long Term Plan's vision for joined-up care by ensuring all professionals have access to consistent, comprehensive information about the people they serve. ## Document Overview This Digital Adaptation Kit provides implementation guidance for the Core Information Standard Version 2.0. It includes clinical terminology mappings, technical specifications, and practical recommendations for system developers and implementers. The kit is organised to support phased implementation, beginning with foundational elements and progressing through increasingly complex information requirements.
