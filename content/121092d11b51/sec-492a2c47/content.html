## Key Health Interventions The Core Information Standard supports evidence-based healthcare delivery through structured data capture aligned with clinical best practices. The following interventions represent priority areas for standardised information management: | Intervention | Recommendation | Source | Grade | |---|---|---|---| | Person-centred care planning | Implement structured goal-setting and outcome tracking using standardised templates | NICE NG86 | A | | Medication reconciliation | Capture complete medication history including over-the-counter medications using dm+d codes | NICE NG5 | A | | Risk assessment documentation | Standardise risk documentation using SNOMED CT codes for consistency across care settings | CQC guidance | B | | Individual requirements capture | Document reasonable adjustments and accessibility needs systematically | Equality Act 2010 | Legal requirement | | Advance care planning | Record preferences for future care including CPR decisions and preferred place of care | GMC guidance | B | | Family history recording | Capture relevant family medical history using structured clinical codes | NICE CG164 | C | | Safeguarding documentation | Maintain comprehensive safeguarding records with appropriate access controls | Care Act 2014 | Legal requirement | | Contact information management | Ensure accurate professional and personal contact details for care coordination | NHS Long Term Plan | B | These interventions are supported by the standard's comprehensive data model, enabling consistent capture and sharing of critical health information across care boundaries.
