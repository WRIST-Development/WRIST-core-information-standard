## Purpose and Scope The Core Information Standard v2.0 (CISV2) defines essential health and care information elements that should be consistently recorded and shared across UK health and social care settings. This standard supports integrated care delivery by establishing a comprehensive framework for capturing person-centred information, from basic demographics through complex care planning and end-of-life preferences. The standard encompasses mandatory person demographics, GP practice details, and extends to include personal preferences, individual requirements, safeguarding considerations, and comprehensive health records. It emphasises person-centred care through dedicated sections like "About Me" that capture what matters most to individuals receiving care. ## Background Developed through extensive stakeholder engagement across health and social care sectors, CISV2 builds upon previous NHS information standards while incorporating contemporary requirements for integrated care systems. The standard addresses the critical need for consistent information capture and sharing, supporting the NHS Long Term Plan's vision for joined-up care delivery. This standard aligns with UK Core FHIR R4 profiles and leverages established clinical terminologies including SNOMED CT, dm+d for medications, and NHS Data Dictionary elements. It provides a structured approach to documenting everything from basic administrative data to complex care plans, ensuring information can flow seamlessly between different care settings while maintaining clinical safety and data quality standards.
