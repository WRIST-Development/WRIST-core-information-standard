## Purpose and Scope The Core Information Standard provides a comprehensive framework for capturing and sharing essential person-centred information across health and social care settings. This standard defines the core data elements necessary to support integrated care delivery, ensuring that critical information about an individual's demographics, care needs, preferences, and circumstances is consistently recorded and accessible to all professionals involved in their care. The standard encompasses fundamental information categories including person demographics, care preferences through the 'About Me' section, individual requirements for reasonable adjustments, alerts, legal information, safeguarding considerations, and comprehensive health and care records. It supports both clinical and social care contexts, promoting a holistic view of the person that extends beyond medical conditions to include their personal preferences, social circumstances, and individual needs. ## Background Developed through extensive stakeholder consultation, the Core Information Standard addresses the longstanding challenge of fragmented information across health and social care systems. It recognises that effective care coordination requires not just clinical data, but also understanding of the person's individual circumstances, preferences, and support needs. The standard incorporates requirements from the Equality Act 2010 for reasonable adjustments and aligns with mental capacity and safeguarding legislation to ensure comprehensive capture of legal and protective information.
