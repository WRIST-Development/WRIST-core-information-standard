## Key Health Interventions The Core Information Standard v2.0 supports documentation of comprehensive health interventions across multiple care domains. The following table summarises key intervention categories and their implementation recommendations: | Intervention Category | Recommendation | Source | Grade | |---|---|---|---| | **Reasonable Adjustments** | Record all adjustments required under Equality Act 2010, including communication needs, mobility support, and sensory accommodations | Equality Act 2010, NHS Accessible Information Standard | Mandatory | | **Medication Management** | Document all medications using dm+d codes, including dose directions, route, and medication changes with clinical rationale | NICE Medicines Optimisation Guidelines | High | | **Vaccination Records** | Capture vaccine product, procedure, site, route, and any adverse reactions using SNOMED CT terminology | UK Health Security Agency Guidelines | High | | **Care Planning** | Implement person-centred care plans documenting goals, actions, outcomes, and review dates with agreement from person/representative | NICE Person-Centred Care Guidelines | High | | **Safeguarding Measures** | Record safeguarding concerns, mental capacity assessments, DoLS applications, and child protection plans with professional accountability | Care Act 2014, Mental Capacity Act 2005 | Mandatory | | **End of Life Care** | Document CPR decisions, advance care planning, preferred place of death, and anticipatory medications | NICE End of Life Care Guidelines | High | | **Risk Management** | Identify and document risks to self/others with mitigation strategies and regular review | NHS Risk Management Framework | Medium | | **Social Care Interventions** | Record services, equipment, adaptations, and support packages with funding sources and review arrangements | Care Act 2014 Guidance | High | ## Implementation Context These interventions should be recorded with full professional accountability, including performer details, dates, locations (using ODS codes), and regular review arrangements. The standard emphasises capturing both clinical and social care interventions to support integrated care delivery.
