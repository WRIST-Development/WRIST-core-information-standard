## Purpose and Scope The Core Information Standard version 2.0 defines the essential information requirements for person-centred health and care records across the UK. This standard ensures that critical information about individuals receiving care is consistently captured, maintained and shared across different care settings and organisations. The standard covers demographic details, clinical information, care preferences, legal status, and support needs - providing a comprehensive framework for holistic person-centred care delivery. ## Background Developed through extensive stakeholder engagement with health and care professionals, patients and carers, this standard addresses the fundamental need for consistent, high-quality information sharing. It supports integrated care delivery by ensuring all professionals have access to the same core information about the people they support. The standard aligns with UK health and care policy initiatives including integrated care systems, personalised care, and digital transformation programmes. It incorporates requirements from the Equality Act 2010, Mental Capacity Act 2005, and other relevant legislation.
