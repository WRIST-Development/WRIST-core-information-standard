## Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides implementation guidance for the Core Information Standard v2.0, a comprehensive data model designed to support person-centred care across health and social care settings in the UK. The standard facilitates the consistent capture, sharing, and use of essential personal information to improve care coordination and outcomes. The Core Information Standard encompasses a wide range of data elements including person demographics, clinical information, care planning, social context, and administrative details. It supports the recording of both structured and unstructured data, enabling healthcare professionals to maintain comprehensive records while ensuring interoperability between systems. ## Background The standard was developed using WRIST (Wonderful Ramsey Systems Information Specification Tool) to ensure robust data modelling and validation. It aligns with NHS Data Dictionary standards, FHIR R4 profiles, and established clinical terminologies including SNOMED CT, LOINC, and dm+d. This DAK serves as a practical guide for organisations implementing the standard, providing technical specifications, clinical guidance, and practical examples to support successful deployment across diverse healthcare environments.
