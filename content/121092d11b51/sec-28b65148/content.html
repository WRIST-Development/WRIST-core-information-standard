## User Personas The following personas represent key stakeholder groups who interact with the Core Information Standard: | Persona ID | Title | Description | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---| | CISV2.P1 | General Practitioner | Primary care physician responsible for comprehensive patient care | Clinical decision-making, care coordination, medication management, referral pathways | | CISV2.P2 | Community Nurse | Registered nurse providing care in community settings | Patient assessment, care planning, medication administration, health promotion | | CISV2.P3 | Social Care Worker | Professional supporting individuals with social care needs | Needs assessment, care planning, safeguarding, multi-agency working | | CISV2.P4 | Hospital Consultant | Specialist physician providing secondary care services | Specialist clinical expertise, complex case management, multidisciplinary team leadership | | CISV2.P5 | Care Home Manager | Manager overseeing residential care facility operations | Staff management, regulatory compliance, resident care oversight, family communication | | CISV2.P6 | Mental Health Professional | Specialist providing mental health assessment and treatment | Mental health assessment, therapeutic interventions, risk management, recovery planning | | CISV2.P7 | Administrative Staff | Personnel managing healthcare records and appointments | Data entry, record maintenance, appointment scheduling, information governance | | CISV2.P8 | Patient/Service User | Individual receiving healthcare and social care services | Self-advocacy, care preferences communication, treatment compliance, feedback provision | **CISV2.P1 (General Practitioner)** requires comprehensive access to all patient information domains to provide holistic primary care and coordinate with other services effectively. **CISV2.P2 (Community Nurse)** focuses on care delivery and monitoring, requiring detailed access to care plans, medications, and assessment data. **CISV2.P8 (Patient/Service User)** represents the central focus of person-centred care, with information captured reflecting their preferences, needs, and circumstances.
