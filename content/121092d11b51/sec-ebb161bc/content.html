## Key User Personas The Core Information Standard serves diverse users across health and social care settings: | Persona ID | Title | Description | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---| | CISV2.P1 | GP/Primary Care Clinician | Provides comprehensive primary healthcare | Clinical assessment, care coordination, referral management | | CISV2.P2 | Hospital Consultant | Specialist medical practitioner in secondary care | Specialist clinical expertise, complex case management | | CISV2.P3 | Community Nurse | Delivers nursing care in community settings | Community health assessment, medication management | | CISV2.P4 | Social Worker | Assesses and coordinates social care services | Needs assessment, safeguarding, care planning | | CISV2.P5 | Care Home Manager | Oversees residential care provision | Care quality management, staff coordination | | CISV2.P6 | Mental Health Practitioner | Provides mental health assessment and treatment | Mental health assessment, risk management, therapeutic interventions | | CISV2.P7 | Service User/Patient | Individual receiving care and support | Self-advocacy, care preferences communication | | CISV2.P8 | Informal Carer | Family member/friend providing unpaid care | Care coordination, advocacy, emotional support | **GP/Primary Care Clinician** serves as the primary healthcare coordinator, requiring comprehensive access to person demographics, medical history, and care plans. **Social Workers** focus on holistic needs assessment and safeguarding, requiring detailed social context and risk information. **Service Users** need access to their own information and ability to communicate preferences and goals.
