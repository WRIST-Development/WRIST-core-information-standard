with complete details | UK Immunisation Schedule, NICE guidance | A (High evidence) | ## Context for Key Intervention Groups ### Person-Centered Information Capture The standard emphasizes capturing information directly from patients about their preferences, communication needs, and personal circumstances. The "About Me" section represents a paradigm shift from purely clinical documentation to incorporating the patient's voice. This supports shared decision-making and ensures care delivery respects individual values and preferences. ### Legal and Regulatory Compliance Several interventions directly support legal obligations. The reasonable adjustments framework ensures compliance with equality legislation, while mental capacity and safeguarding documentation meet statutory requirements. These elements must be implemented as mandatory fields with appropriate access controls and audit trails. ### Clinical Safety and Quality The comprehensive capture of allergies, medications, and clinical alerts directly supports patient safety initiatives. By ensuring this information travels with the patient across care settings, the risk of adverse events is significantly reduced. The standard mandates structured recording using approved terminologies (dm+d for medications, SNOMED CT for clinical concepts) to minimize ambiguity. ### Integrated Care Delivery Care planning sections enable multidisciplinary teams to coordinate interventions effectively. By documenting goals, actions, and responsible parties in a structured format, the standard supports the delivery of integrated care pathways. This is particularly important for patients with complex needs who receive support from multiple services. ### Population Health Management Systematic capture of social determinants, lifestyle factors, and risk assessments enables population-level analysis and targeted interventions. The standard includes fields for occupation, housing status, and social circumstances that are often missing from traditional clinical systems but crucial for addressing health inequalities.
