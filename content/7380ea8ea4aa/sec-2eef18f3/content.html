## Overview The Core Information Standard v2.0 defines essential information management interventions that support safe, effective, and person-centered care delivery. These interventions focus on systematic capture, maintenance, and sharing of core patient information across health and care settings. ## Key Interventions Summary | Intervention | Recommendation | Source/Guideline | Evidence Grade | |---|---|---|---| | **Comprehensive Identity Verification** | Verify patient identity using NHS number and minimum two demographic identifiers at every care interaction | NHS Patient Safety Standards, NICE Guidelines | A - Mandatory requirement | | **Contact Information Validation** | Validate and update contact information at every scheduled encounter | Information Governance Standards | B - Moderate recommendation | ## Detailed Intervention Descriptions ### Identity Management Interventions The standard mandates robust identity verification processes to prevent patient misidentification - a never event in healthcare. Key requirements include: - **Primary identifier**: NHS number (or equivalent national identifier) must be verified against national systems - **Demographic matching**: Minimum of name, date of birth, and address must be confirmed - **Biometric options**: Photograph capture recommended for enhanced identification ### Clinical Information Interventions #### Problem List Maintenance - Maintain an accurate, coded problem list using SNOMED CT terminology - Review and update at every clinical encounter - Include both active and resolved conditions with appropriate status indicators #### Medication Management - Document all current medications including over-the-counter and complementary therapies - Use dm+d coding for all prescribed items - Include dose, route, frequency, and indication for each medication - Record medication allergies and intolerances with reaction details #### Assessment Documentation - Conduct and document standardized assessments for: - Cognitive function (where indicated) - Mobility and functional status - Social circumstances and support needs - Mental health and wellbeing ### Person-Centered Interventions #### "About Me" Documentation - Capture patient preferences, values, and what matters most to them - Document communication needs and preferences - Record how the person wishes to be supported - Update regularly through patient/carer involvement #### Reasonable Adjustments - Systematically identify needs for: - Communication support (e.g., interpreters, easy-read materials) - Physical adjustments (e.g., accessible facilities, longer appointments) - Sensory adjustments (e.g., hearing loops, large print) - Flag adjustments prominently in clinical systems ### Safeguarding Interventions - Screen for safeguarding risks including: - Domestic abuse - Child protection concerns - Adult safeguarding needs - Mental health crisis indicators - Document with appropriate confidentiality controls - Establish clear escalation pathways ### Legal and Ethical Interventions #### Consent Management - Document informed consent for information sharing - Record treatment preferences and limitations - Capture advance decisions and living wills - Identify appointed decision-makers (LPA, deputies) #### Mental Capacity Assessment - Assess and document capacity for specific decisions - Record best interest decisions where capacity is lacking - Review capacity status regularly as conditions change ## Implementation Considerations Successful implementation requires: - Staff training on all intervention protocols - Clinical decision support tools embedded in workflows - Regular audit and feedback on compliance - Patient and carer engagement in design and testing - Phased rollout with continuous improvement cycles
