providing advice | Yes | Medication knowledge, interaction checking, patient counseling, prescribing systems | ## Detailed Persona Descriptions ### P001 - General Practitioner Dr. Sarah Chen is an experienced GP managing a patient list of 1,800 individuals. She conducts 30-40 consultations daily, balancing acute presentations with chronic disease management. She needs rapid access to comprehensive patient information during 10-minute appointments, including alerts, current medications, and recent hospital contacts. She is responsible for maintaining accurate problem lists, conducting medication reviews, and ensuring coding meets QOF requirements. Technology comfort is high, but she is frustrated by multiple logins and duplicate data entry. ### P002 - Practice Nurse James Thompson is a practice nurse specializing in diabetes and respiratory care. He conducts structured reviews, administers vaccinations, and manages recall systems. He needs templates that guide comprehensive assessments while allowing free-text capture of patient concerns. He often identifies social issues affecting health and needs clear pathways for referral. He is confident with clinical systems but requires intuitive interfaces that don't slow patient interaction. ### P003 - Community Nurse Maria Rodriguez leads a community nursing team covering a diverse urban area. She visits 8-10 patients daily, many with complex comorbidities and social challenges. She needs mobile access to care plans, medication lists, and contact details for other professionals. Network connectivity is variable, requiring offline capability. She coordinates with multiple agencies and needs to document detailed assessments while maintaining patient rapport. ### P004 - Social Worker David Patel specializes in adult safeguarding and mental capacity assessments. He works across health and social care boundaries, often accessing multiple systems. He needs to document detailed assessments, record multi-agency discussions, and track complex legal statuses. Information governance is paramount, with careful control over information sharing. He requires systems that support narrative documentation while extracting structured data for reporting. ### P005 - Hospital Doctor Dr. Amara Okafor is a medical registrar managing acute admissions. She sees 15-20 patients daily across wards and emergency departments. She needs immediate access to GP summaries, recent investigations, and advance care plans. Time pressure is intense, requiring efficient navigation and clear presentation of critical information. She must document thoroughly for legal and handover purposes while managing clinical priorities. ### P006 - Allied Health Professional Tom Williams is a physiotherapist working across community and outpatient settings. He conducts functional assessments, develops rehabilitation plans, and measures outcomes. He needs to document structured assessments while capturing patient goals and preferences. Integration with broader care plans is essential, as is communication with referring clinicians. He uses profession-specific outcome measures requiring flexible data capture. ### P007 - Care Coordinator Priya Sharma coordinates care for patients with severe mental illness across multiple providers. She maintains oversight of 60 complex cases, tracking appointments, medication compliance, and crisis plans. She needs dashboards showing patient status, upcoming reviews, and outstanding actions. She facilitates multi-disciplinary meetings requiring real-time information access and update capabilities. ### P008 - Administrator Karen Foster manages patient registration and data quality for a large practice. She processes 20-30 registrations weekly, ensures demographic accuracy, and handles access requests. She needs clear workflows for data entry, validation tools to prevent errors, and reports highlighting data quality issues. She has limited clinical knowledge but deep understanding of information governance requirements. ### P009 - Patient/Carer Robert and Margaret Wilson are in their 70s, managing multiple conditions with increasing digital engagement. They want access to their records, ability to update preferences, and clear information about their care plans. Digital confidence varies, requiring intuitive interfaces with good accessibility features. They value security but struggle with complex authentication processes. ### P010 - Pharmacist Aisha Patel is a clinical pharmacist conducting medication reviews in primary care. She needs comprehensive medication histories, allergy information, and recent biochemistry results. She identifies optimization opportunities and medicines-related problems. She requires detailed documentation capabilities and integration with prescribing systems. She often identifies adherence issues requiring sensitive documentation.
