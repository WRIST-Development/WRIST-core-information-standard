## Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides comprehensive guidance for implementing the **Core Information Standard v2.0** across digital health systems. The standard establishes a foundational framework for capturing, storing, and sharing essential person-centered information in health and care settings. The Core Information Standard serves as a universal baseline for patient information management, ensuring that critical data elements are consistently recorded and accessible across the care continuum. This DAK translates the standard's requirements into actionable technical specifications for system developers, implementers, and healthcare organizations. ## Background and Context ### Health Domain Overview The fragmentation of health information systems represents a significant challenge to delivering coordinated, person-centered care. Studies indicate that incomplete or inaccessible patient information contributes to approximately 10% of patient safety incidents and significantly impacts care quality and efficiency. In the UK context, where this standard originates, the NHS serves over 66 million people with care delivered across multiple settings - from GP practices and community services to acute hospitals and social care providers. The lack of standardized information exchange between these settings creates barriers to continuity of care, particularly for individuals with complex needs who interact with multiple services. ### Policy Context The Core Information Standard aligns with several key policy initiatives: - **NHS Long Term Plan**: Emphasizes the need for interoperable digital systems that support integrated care - **Data Strategy for Health and Social Care**: Promotes standardized data capture and sharing to improve outcomes - **Person-Centered Care Framework**: Ensures information systems support individual preferences and needs ## Relationship to Clinical Guidelines While the Core Information Standard is not a clinical guideline itself, it provides the informational foundation that enables adherence to clinical best practices. It ensures that: 1. **Essential demographic and contact information** is available for patient identification and communication 2. **Clinical history and current health status** can be accurately documented and shared 3. **Person preferences and requirements** are captured to support individualized care delivery 4. **Legal and safeguarding information** is recorded to ensure appropriate protections ## Document Overview This DAK is structured according to WHO SMART Guidelines methodology, providing: - **Health interventions and recommendations** derived from the standard's requirements - **Generic personas** representing key user groups - **User scenarios** illustrating practical applications - **Business processes and workflows** for implementation - **Core data elements** with detailed specifications - **Decision support logic** for data validation and clinical rules - **Indicators and metrics** for monitoring implementation - **Functional and non-functional requirements** for system design - **Implementation references and resources**
