## Scenario 1: New Patient Registration with Complex Needs ### Narrative Mrs. Jamila Hassan, a 68-year-old woman with moderate dementia and type 2 diabetes, has recently moved to live with her daughter following her husband's death. She arrives at the GP practice for registration accompanied by her daughter Amina, who has lasting power of attorney for health and welfare. Mrs. Hassan speaks limited English and requires a Somali interpreter for healthcare consultations. She uses a wheelchair following a stroke two years ago and has a history of medication non-compliance. ### Key Data Elements | Category | Description | |---|---| | Demographics | Name, DOB, ethnicity, preferred language (Somali), interpreter requirements | | Contact Details | New address, daughter as primary contact, multiple phone numbers | | Legal Status | LPA documentation, capacity assessment for medical decisions | | Clinical Alerts | Dementia diagnosis, stroke history, diabetes, medication compliance issues | | Reasonable Adjustments | Interpreter needs, wheelchair access, longer appointments, written materials in Somali | | Social Context | Recent bereavement, living with family, cultural preferences | ### Personas Involved - P008 (Administrator) - Initial registration and demographic data entry - P001 (GP) - Clinical assessment and care planning - P002 (Practice Nurse) - Diabetes review and care coordination - P009 (Patient/Carer) - Amina providing information and consent ### Corresponding Business Process - BP001: Patient Registration and Initial Assessment ## Scenario 2: Multi-Disciplinary Care Planning for Frequent Hospital Attender ### Narrative Mr. Thomas Murphy, 55, has had six emergency admissions in the past year related to alcohol dependency, depression, and unstable housing. A case conference is arranged involving hospital staff, his GP, community mental health team, and housing support worker. ### Key Data Elements | Category | Description | |---|---| | Problem List | Alcohol dependency, depression, epilepsy, homelessness risk | | Service Contacts | Multiple teams involved, attendance patterns, key worker details | | Risk Assessments | Self-neglect, vulnerability, seizure management | | Care Plans | Crisis plan, alcohol reduction strategy, housing support | | Medications | Anti-epileptics, antidepressants, vitamin supplementation, adherence issues | | Social Circumstances | Unstable housing, limited family support, unemployment | ### Personas Involved - P005 (Hospital Doctor) - Discharge planning and medical management - P001 (GP) - Primary care coordination and prescription management - P004 (Social Worker) - Housing assessment and vulnerability support - P007 (Care Coordinator) - MDT facilitation and plan monitoring ### Corresponding Business Process - BP003: Multi-Disciplinary Team Care Planning ## Scenario 3: End of Life Care Preferences Documentation ### Narrative Mr. William Chen, 78, has advanced COPD and has been identified for palliative care following recent ICU admission. During a home visit, the community palliative care nurse discusses his preferences for future care. Mr. Chen expresses clear wishes about preferring comfort care at home, declining further intensive interventions, and wanting his son involved in all decisions. He has capacity and wants to formally document an advance decision to refuse treatment. ### Key Data Elements | Category | Description | |---|---| | Prognosis | Estimated months of life, disease trajectory | | Advance Care Planning | Preferred place of death (home), DNACPR decision, treatment preferences | | Legal Documentation | ADRT for ventilation/ICU, capacity assessment recorded | | Family Involvement | Son as primary contact, family awareness documented | | Anticipatory Medications | Just-in-case box location, authorization details | | Service Coordination | Hospice at home contact, GP awareness, out-of-hours handover | ### Personas Involved - P003 (Community Nurse) - Palliative care assessment and preference documentation - P001 (GP) - DNACPR completion and medication prescribing - P009 (Patient/Carer) - Mr. Chen and son expressing and confirming wishes - P010 (Pharmacist) - Anticipatory medication supply and counseling ### Corresponding Business Process - BP005: Advance Care Planning Documentation ## Scenario 4: Safeguarding Concern Identification and Referral ### Narrative During a routine diabetic foot check, Practice Nurse notices unexplained bruising on Mrs. Dorothy Parker, an 82-year-old with mild cognitive impairment. Mrs. Parker seems anxious and mentions her grandson has been "helping with finances" since her husband died. She appears unkempt, having previously taken pride in appearance, and mentions she "doesn't have money for the hairdresser anymore." ### Key Data Elements | Category | Description | |---|---| | Safeguarding Indicators | Physical signs (bruising), behavioral changes, financial concerns | | Capacity Assessment | Mild cognitive impairment but retains capacity for daily decisions | | Risk Factors | Recent bereavement, social isolation, family member managing finances | | Professional Actions | Photography of injuries, detailed documentation, GP discussion | | Referral Information | Adult social services referral, information sharing decisions | | Follow-up Plans | GP review appointment, social work assessment arranged | ### Personas Involved - P002 (Practice Nurse) - Initial identification and escalation - P001 (GP) - Clinical assessment and safeguarding referral - P004 (Social Worker) - Safeguarding investigation and support planning - P008 (Administrator) - Appointment coordination and referral processing ### Corresponding Business Process - BP004: Safeguarding Identification and Referral ## Scenario 5: Transition from Pediatric to Adult Services ### Narrative Alex Johnson, turning 18 next month, has cerebral palsy, learning disabilities, and epilepsy.
