## Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides comprehensive guidance for the implementation of the Core Information Standard version 2.0 within digital health systems. The Core Information Standard represents a foundational framework for capturing, storing, and exchanging essential patient information across health and care settings in the United Kingdom. This DAK translates the clinical and informational requirements of the standard into actionable technical specifications suitable for digital implementation. The scope of this document encompasses the complete lifecycle of patient information management, from initial demographic data capture through to complex care planning and end-of-life considerations. It addresses the needs of diverse healthcare providers including general practitioners, specialists, nurses, social workers, and allied health professionals working across primary, secondary, and community care settings. ## Background and Context The UK healthcare system faces increasing pressure to deliver integrated, patient-centered care across multiple providers and settings. Fragmented information systems have historically created barriers to effective care coordination, leading to duplicated efforts, missed opportunities for intervention, and potential patient safety risks. The NHS Long Term Plan emphasizes the critical importance of interoperable digital systems that support seamless information sharing while maintaining patient privacy and security. The Core Information Standard emerged from extensive consultation with healthcare professionals, patients, and technical experts to identify the essential data elements required for safe and effective care delivery. It builds upon existing NHS data standards while introducing structured approaches to capturing patient preferences, social circumstances, and care planning information that have traditionally been poorly documented in clinical systems. ## Relationship to Clinical Guidelines This DAK supports the implementation of various NICE guidelines and NHS England policies by ensuring that critical patient information is available at the point of care. It particularly aligns with: - NICE guideline NG56 on multimorbidity, which emphasizes the importance of comprehensive assessment and personalized care planning - NHS England's Universal Personalized Care model, requiring systematic capture of patient goals and preferences - The Mental Capacity Act 2005 requirements for documenting capacity assessments and best interest decisions - Equality Act 2010 obligations for recording and acting upon reasonable adjustments The standard does not prescribe clinical pathways but rather ensures that the information needed to follow evidence-based guidelines is consistently available across care settings. ## Document Overview This DAK is structured according to WHO SMART Guidelines methodology, providing: - **Health Interventions & Recommendations**: Key clinical and care activities supported by the standard - **Generic Personas**: Representative users who will interact with systems implementing the standard - **User Scenarios**: Typical workflows demonstrating how the standard supports care delivery - **Business Processes**: Detailed process maps showing information flow and decision points - **Core Data Elements**: Comprehensive data dictionary with validation rules and terminology bindings - **Decision Support Logic**: Automated rules for data quality, clinical safety alerts, and care coordination - **Indicators & Metrics**: Measurable outcomes for monitoring implementation success - **Requirements**: Functional and non-functional specifications for system implementation Each section builds upon the previous, creating a complete picture of how the Core Information Standard transforms from policy intent to operational reality within digital health systems.
