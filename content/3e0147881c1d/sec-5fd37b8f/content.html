## Overview While the Core Information Standard itself is not a clinical intervention, it fundamentally enables and supports the delivery of safe, effective health and care interventions. This section outlines how comprehensive core information supports evidence-based care delivery and key recommendations for its implementation. ## Information-Enabled Interventions The availability of complete core information directly supports the following intervention categories: ### Emergency and Urgent Care - Rapid access to allergies, medications, and advance directives - Immediate identification of safeguarding concerns - Access to resuscitation status and end-of-life preferences - Contact details for next of kin and care coordinators ### Coordinated Care Planning - Holistic assessment informed by social context and preferences - Person-centered goal setting based on "About Me" information - Multi-disciplinary team coordination with shared information - Care transitions supported by complete handover information ### Reasonable Adjustments - Communication needs (interpreters, easy-read materials) - Mobility and access requirements - Cognitive support needs - Sensory impairments requiring accommodation ### Medication Safety - Complete medication history across all prescribers - Allergy and adverse reaction information - Drug interaction checking with full medication list - Adherence support based on person's capabilities ## Key Recommendations | Intervention | Recommendation | Source/Guideline | Evidence Grade | |--------------|----------------|------------------|----------------| | Information Capture | Capture core information at first contact with health/care services | PRSB Standards | Expert Consensus | | Information Sharing | Share core information at all care transitions and handovers | NHS Digital Interoperability Standards | Best Practice | | Person-Centered Recording | Co-produce "About Me" sections with person/carer involvement | NICE Guidelines on Person-Centered Care | Strong | | Reasonable Adjustments | Record and flag all required adjustments per Equality Act 2010 | Equality Act 2010, NHS Accessible Information Standard | Legislative Requirement | | Consent Documentation | Document explicit consent for information sharing with clear scope | UK GDPR, Common Law Duty of Confidentiality | Legal Requirement | | Safeguarding Flags | Prominently display safeguarding concerns and contact details | Safeguarding Vulnerable Groups Act 2006 | Legislative Requirement | | Regular Review | Review and update core information at least annually or at major care transitions | PRSB Core Information Standard | Expert Consensus | | Emergency Access | Ensure emergency services can access critical information 24/7 | NHS Urgent and Emergency Care Standards | Best Practice | ## Information Quality Interventions To ensure core information effectively supports care delivery: ### Data Completeness - Mandatory fields must be completed before record closure - Regular audits of information completeness - Prompts for missing critical information - Integration with national registries (PDS, PAS) ### Data Accuracy - Regular verification with person/carer - Professional authentication of clinical information - Audit trails for all changes - Reconciliation processes at care transitions ### Data Timeliness - Real-time updates for critical information - Dated entries for time-sensitive information - Alerts for information requiring review - Synchronization across care settings ## Supporting Evidence The recommendations above are supported by: - **Clinical Evidence**: Studies showing reduced adverse events with complete medication lists - **Patient Experience**: Improved satisfaction when preferences are known and respected - **Efficiency Gains**: Reduced duplication of assessments and questions - **Safety Outcomes**: Fewer "Never Events" with accessible allergy/alert information - **Legal Compliance**: Meeting duties under Equality Act, Mental Capacity Act, and safeguarding legislation
