## Purpose and Scope The Core Information Standard (CIS) Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides comprehensive guidance for implementing digital systems that capture, store, and share essential person-centered health and care information across the UK health and social care system. This DAK translates the Core Information Standard v2.0 into actionable technical specifications for digital health implementers. The Core Information Standard represents a foundational framework for ensuring that critical information about a person follows them throughout their health and care journey. It encompasses demographic details, clinical information, social care needs, legal status, and person-centered preferences that enable safe, effective, and coordinated care delivery. ## Background and Context ### The Challenge of Fragmented Information Across health and social care settings, information about individuals is often scattered across multiple systems, organizations, and paper records. This fragmentation leads to: - Repeated requests for the same information from patients and carers - Critical information being unavailable at the point of care - Increased risk of medical errors and safeguarding issues - Poor care coordination between services - Frustration for both care recipients and providers ### Policy Context The Core Information Standard aligns with key UK health and care policies: - **NHS Long Term Plan**: Supporting integrated care systems and joined-up care delivery - **Data Saves Lives**: Ensuring data is used effectively to improve health outcomes - **Integration White Paper**: Breaking down barriers between health and social care - **Equality Act 2010**: Ensuring reasonable adjustments are recorded and communicated The standard has been developed through extensive consultation with patients, carers, health professionals, social care workers, and digital health experts across the UK. ## Relationship to Clinical Guidelines and SMART Guidelines While the Core Information Standard is not a clinical guideline per se, it provides the information infrastructure necessary to support evidence-based care delivery. It ensures that: - Clinical decisions are informed by complete, accurate information - Care plans and interventions consider the whole person - Safety-critical information is always available - Person-centered preferences guide care delivery This DAK follows WHO SMART Guidelines principles by providing: - Structured data models for interoperability - Decision support logic for information validation - Clear workflows for information capture and sharing - Measurable indicators for data quality and completeness ## Document Structure This DAK is organized into the following sections: 1. **Health Interventions & Recommendations**: While not prescribing specific clinical interventions, outlines how the standard supports safe care delivery 2. **Generic Personas**: Key user types who interact with core information 3. **User Scenarios**: Real-world situations demonstrating information needs 4. **Business Processes & Workflows**: How information flows through care pathways 5. **Core Data Elements**: Detailed specifications for each information component 6. **Decision-Support Logic**: Validation rules and information quality checks 7. **Indicators & Performance Metrics**: Measuring standard adoption and impact 8. **Functional & Non-Functional Requirements**: System capabilities needed 9. **References**: Source documents and standards
