## Overview The Core Information Standard serves diverse users across health and social care settings. These personas represent key user groups who create, access, update, and rely on core information in their daily work. Each persona has distinct needs, competencies, and interaction patterns with the standard. ## Persona Summary Table | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key Competencies | |------------|-------------------|-------------|----------|------------------| | CIS.P1 | General Practitioner (GP) | Primary care physician responsible for ongoing care coordination | Yes | Clinical assessment, diagnosis, prescribing, care planning, EHR systems | | CIS.P2 | Community Nurse | Provides nursing care in community settings including homes | Yes | Clinical assessment, medication administration, wound care, mobile technology | | CIS.P3 | Social Worker | Coordinates social care services and safeguarding | Yes | Needs assessment, safeguarding, care planning, case management systems | | CIS.P4 | Emergency Department Clinician | Provides urgent/emergency care in acute settings | Yes | Rapid assessment, emergency procedures, critical decision-making, emergency systems | | CIS.P5 | Care Home Manager | Oversees residential care facility operations | Yes | Care coordination, staff management, regulatory compliance, care management systems | | CIS.P6 | Person/Patient | Individual receiving health and care services | Yes | Self-advocacy, basic digital literacy, understanding own needs | | CIS.P7 | Family Carer | Provides unpaid care to family member | Yes | Personal care, advocacy, basic health monitoring, smartphone/tablet use | | CIS.P8 | Paramedic | Provides emergency pre-hospital care | Yes | Emergency assessment, life support, mobile data access, communication | | CIS.P9 | Hospital Discharge Coordinator | Manages transitions from hospital to community | Yes | Discharge planning, multi-agency coordination, risk assessment, IT systems | | CIS.P10 | Pharmacist | Manages medications and provides pharmaceutical care | Yes | Medication review, drug interactions, prescribing systems, counseling | ## Detailed Persona Descriptions ### CIS.P1: General Practitioner (GP) Dr. Sarah Chen is a GP partner in a busy urban practice serving 12,000 patients. She sees 30-40 patients daily, mixing face-to-face and remote consultations. Her practice uses a major GP system (EMIS/SystmOne) integrated with local hospital systems. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Reviews and updates medical history, medications, and allergies - Records new diagnoses and updates problem lists - Creates and monitors care plans - Coordinates with secondary care and social services - Ensures reasonable adjustments are documented **Pain Points:** - Information from hospitals arrives in different formats - Social care information often missing - Time pressure limits thorough record updates ### CIS.P2: Community Nurse James Thompson is a district nurse covering a rural area, visiting 8-12 patients daily in their homes. He uses a tablet device with mobile access to patient records and often works in areas with poor connectivity. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Accesses care plans and "About Me" information before visits - Updates observations and wound assessments - Records medication administration - Identifies and escalates safeguarding concerns - Documents environmental risks for other visitors **Pain Points:** - Offline access needed for remote locations - Different systems between NHS and local authority - Critical information buried in lengthy records ### CIS.P3: Social Worker Maria Gonzalez is an adult social worker specializing in mental health. She manages a caseload of 25 clients with complex needs, coordinating between health services, housing, and benefits agencies. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Conducts comprehensive social care assessments - Records safeguarding concerns and actions - Documents mental capacity assessments - Updates personal circumstances and support networks - Creates contingency plans for crisis situations **Pain Points:** - Limited access to NHS clinical systems - Consent processes complex for information sharing - Difficulty tracking multi-agency involvement ### CIS.P4: Emergency Department Clinician Dr. Michael O'Brien works in a major trauma center ED seeing 20-30 patients per shift. He needs rapid access to critical information for clinical decision-making under time pressure. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Rapid review of allergies, medications, and alerts - Checks advance directives and resuscitation status - Identifies next of kin and lasting power of attorney - Reviews recent investigations and diagnoses - Updates problem list with new presentations **Pain Points:** - Information overload in emergency situations - Critical details not prominently displayed - Systems slow during peak times ### CIS.P5: Care Home Manager Susan Patel manages a 60-bed residential care home for older adults. She oversees care delivery, staff training, and regulatory compliance while maintaining person-centered care standards. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Maintains comprehensive resident profiles - Ensures staff aware of individual needs and preferences - Coordinates with GPs and community services - Manages medication administration records - Documents incidents and safeguarding concerns **Pain Points:** - Paper-based information from some health services - Staff need quick access during personal care - Keeping information synchronized across agencies ### CIS.P6: Person/Patient Robert Williams is 72, living with diabetes, mild cognitive impairment, and mobility issues. His daughter helps with appointments but he values maintaining independence. He uses a smartphone for video calls but finds complex apps challenging. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Contributes to "About Me" section - Reviews and corrects information - Provides consent for information sharing - Accesses own record through patient portal - Updates preferences and emergency contacts **Pain Points:** - Multiple passwords for different systems - Medical jargon difficult to understand - Concerns about who sees personal information ### CIS.P7: Family Carer Aisha Mohammed cares for her mother who has dementia while working part-time. She coordinates multiple appointments, manages medications, and advocates for her mother's needs across services. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Updates contact details and availability - Contributes to "About Me" on mother's behalf - Accesses care plans and appointment schedules - Records observations about changes in condition - Receives alerts about important updates **Pain Points:** - Proving authority to access information - Remembering what each professional has been told - Coordinating information from multiple sources ### CIS.P8: Paramedic Dave Roberts responds to 999 calls across the region, attending medical emergencies in homes and public places. He uses vehicle-mounted and handheld devices to access patient information at scene. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Rapid access to summary information - Reviews medications and allergies - Checks advance care plans and DNACPR - Identifies key contacts and usual care providers - Documents observations and interventions **Pain Points:** - Connectivity issues in rural areas - Time pressure prevents detailed review - Different regions use different systems ### CIS.P9: Hospital Discharge Coordinator Lisa Chang coordinates complex discharges for patients with ongoing care needs. She liaises between ward teams, community services, social care, and families to ensure safe transitions. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Compiles discharge summaries - Updates care plans and medication lists - Ensures follow-up appointments documented - Coordinates equipment and adaptations - Shares information with receiving services **Pain Points:** - Gathering information from multiple hospital systems - Ensuring community services have access - Last-minute changes disrupting plans ### CIS.P10: Pharmacist Kiran Singh works in a community pharmacy providing medication reviews, health advice, and managing prescriptions for local residents. She needs accurate medication histories and allergy information. **Key Interactions with Core Information:** - Reviews complete medication lists - Checks for allergies and interactions - Documents medication reviews and interventions - Provides adherence support based on capabilities - Updates vaccination records **Pain Points:** - Incomplete medication histories - Lack of access to full clinical record - Different prescribing systems not synchronized
