## Overview These user scenarios illustrate real-world situations where the Core Information Standard enables better care delivery. Each scenario demonstrates how different personas interact with core information across various care settings and transitions. ## Scenario 1: Emergency Admission with Complex Needs **Title:** Emergency Response for Person with Learning Disabilities **Narrative:** David, 35, has moderate learning disabilities and epilepsy. He lives in supported accommodation and is found unconscious by his support worker after a seizure. The paramedic crew arrives and needs to quickly understand David's baseline communication abilities, regular medications, and any advance decisions about hospital treatment. **Data Elements:** | Category | Description | |----------|-------------| | Person Demographics | Name, DOB, NHS Number, emergency contacts | | About Me | Communication needs, behavioral triggers, coping strategies | | Reasonable Adjustments | Requires easy-read information, presence of familiar support worker, extra time for procedures | | Medications | Anti-epileptic drugs with dosages and timing | | Allergies | Penicillin allergy with anaphylaxis history | | Professional Contacts | Epilepsy specialist nurse, learning disability team | | Legal Information | Best interests decision-making framework documented | **Personas Involved:** - CIS.P8 (Paramedic) - Accessing emergency information - CIS.P4 (ED Clinician) - Receiving handover and treating - CIS.P7 (Support Worker as Carer) - Providing collateral history - CIS.P3 (Learning Disability Social Worker) - Coordinating reasonable adjustments **Related Business Processes:** - CIS.BP1: Emergency Information Access - CIS.BP4: Reasonable Adjustments Implementation - CIS.BP7: Care Transition Communication ## Scenario 2: Planned Hospital Discharge **Title:** Complex Discharge for Older Person with Multiple Conditions **Narrative:** Margaret, 82, is being discharged after hip surgery. She has diabetes, mild dementia, and lives alone. The discharge coordinator must ensure community services have complete information about her mobility needs, cognitive support requirements, and home environment to arrange appropriate care package. **Data Elements:** | Category | Description | |----------|-------------| | Social Context | Lives alone, daughter visits twice weekly, narrow stairs | | Assessments | Mobility assessment, cognitive assessment, home environment risk | | Services & Care | Existing home care twice daily, meals on wheels | | Equipment | New walking frame, raised toilet seat required | | Medications | Changes to anticoagulation, new pain relief regimen | | Care Plan | Physiotherapy goals, falls prevention strategies | | Contingency Plan | Daughter's contact if carer cannot access property | **Personas Involved:** - CIS.P9 (Discharge Coordinator) - Compiling discharge information - CIS.P2 (Community Nurse) - Receiving care for wound monitoring - CIS.P3 (Social Worker) - Arranging enhanced care package - CIS.P6 (Margaret as Patient) - Expressing preferences - CIS.P7 (Daughter as Carer) - Supporting transition **Related Business Processes:** - CIS.BP8: Discharge Planning - CIS.BP3: Assessment Documentation - CIS.BP6: Care Plan Creation and Update ## Scenario 3: Primary Care Coordination **Title:** Annual Review with Multiple Long-term Conditions **Narrative:** Rajesh, 68, attends his GP for an annual review. He has diabetes, hypertension, and recently diagnosed mild cognitive impairment. The GP needs to review all current treatments, update care plans, check if any reasonable adjustments are needed, and ensure information is shared with relevant specialists. **Data Elements:** | Category | Description | |----------|-------------| | Problem List | Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, mild cognitive impairment | | Medications | Multiple medications requiring review and optimization | | Investigations | Recent HbA1c, blood pressure readings, cognitive assessment | | Care Plans | Diabetes management plan, hypertension monitoring schedule | | About Me | Prefers written instructions, wife helps with medications | | Lifestyle | Struggling with diet changes, reduced physical activity | | Referrals | Memory clinic follow-up, diabetic eye screening due | **Personas Involved:** - CIS.P1 (GP) - Conducting comprehensive review - CIS.P10 (Pharmacist) - Medication optimization - CIS.P6 (Rajesh as Patient) - Discussing concerns - CIS.P7 (Wife as Carer) - Supporting medication adherence **Related Business Processes:** - CIS.BP5: Clinical Review and Update - CIS.BP6: Care Plan Creation and Update - CIS.BP2: Information Sharing with Consent ## Scenario 4: Safeguarding Concern **Title:** Identifying and Managing Safeguarding Risk **Narrative:** Sarah, 45, with mental health issues and learning difficulties, is visited by her community nurse who notices unexplained bruising. The nurse must document concerns, ensure immediate safety, coordinate with safeguarding team, and update all professionals involved in Sarah's care. **Data Elements:** | Category | Description | |----------|-------------| | Safeguarding | New concern raised, photographs taken, body map completed | | Risk Assessment | Vulnerability factors documented | | Mental Capacity | Capacity assessment for understanding safeguarding process | | Professional Network | Mental health team, social worker, GP all alerted | | Investigation Plan | Safeguarding meeting scheduled, interim safety plan | | Legal Status | Existing DoLS in care home reviewed | **Personas Involved:** - CIS.P2 (Community Nurse) - Identifying concern - CIS.P3 (Social Worker) - Leading safeguarding investigation - CIS.P5 (Care Home Manager) - Implementing safety measures - CIS.P1 (GP) - Medical examination and documentation **Related Business Processes:** - CIS.BP9: Safeguarding Recording and Alerts - CIS.BP10: Multi-agency Information Sharing - CIS.BP3: Assessment Documentation ## Scenario 5: End of Life Care **Title:** Coordinating End of Life Preferences **Narrative:** John, 78, with advanced heart failure, wants to plan for end of life care. His GP helps document his preferences for place of death, DNACPR decision, and ensures all services involved in his care have access to this information. **Data Elements:** | Category | Description | |----------|-------------| | End of Life Care | Preferred place of death (home), awareness of prognosis | | Legal Decisions | DNACPR form completed, Lasting Power of Attorney documented | | Anticipatory Medications | Just in case medications prescribed and location recorded | | Care Preferences | Wishes for comfort care only, spiritual needs documented | | Key Contacts | Wife as main contact, son has LPA for health decisions | | Contingency Plans | Out of hours services have access to plans | **Personas Involved:** - CIS.P1 (GP) - Facilitating advance care planning - CIS.P6 (John as Patient) - Expressing wishes - CIS.P7 (Wife as Carer) - Supporting decisions - CIS.P2 (Community Nurse) - Providing ongoing support - CIS.P8 (Paramedic) - May need to access DNACPR status **Related Business Processes:** - CIS.BP11: Advance Care Planning - CIS.BP2: Information Sharing with Consent - CIS.BP1: Emergency Information Access ## Cross-Cutting Themes These scenarios illustrate several key themes: 1. **Information Continuity**: Core information must flow seamlessly between services 2. **Person-Centered Focus**: Individual preferences and needs drive care delivery 3. **Multi-Professional Access**: Different professionals need different views of information 4. **Temporal Aspects**: Information must be current but retain relevant history 5. **Consent and Control**: People must control their information while enabling necessary sharing 6. **Emergency Override**: Critical situations require rapid access to essential information
