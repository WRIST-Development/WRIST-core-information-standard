## Overview of WRIST-Enabled Interventions The WRIST Core Information Standard enables a comprehensive set of health and care interventions through its structured data capture and sharing capabilities. These interventions span preventive care, acute treatment, chronic disease management, mental health support, and social care coordination. The following recommendations are derived from analysis of the standard's 47 concept groups and their alignment with evidence-based practice guidelines. ## Key Intervention Categories ### Person-Centered Care Planning The WRIST standard's emphasis on capturing personal preferences, requirements, and goals enables truly individualized care delivery. The "About Me" and "Individual Requirements" sections provide essential context for tailoring all subsequent interventions. ### Integrated Assessment and Care Coordination By standardizing assessment documentation across health and social care domains, WRIST enables comprehensive needs identification and coordinated response across multiple providers and settings. ### Risk Management and Safeguarding The standard's structured approach to documenting risks, alerts, and safeguarding concerns supports proactive intervention to prevent harm and protect vulnerable individuals. ## Summary of Key Clinical Recommendations | Intervention | Recommendation | Source/Guideline | Evidence Grade | |---|---|---|---| | **Comprehensive Assessment** | Conduct holistic assessment covering physical health, mental health, social circumstances, and personal goals using structured tools | NICE NG56 Multimorbidity; Key assessment interventions include: **Physical Health Assessments**: Utilizing the examination findings and investigation results sections to capture objective health data supporting evidence-based interventions. This includes vital signs monitoring, system-specific examinations, and diagnostic test interpretation. **Mental Health and Cognitive Assessments**: The assessment tools section enables standardized mental health screening and monitoring using validated instruments. Results directly inform care planning and risk management strategies. **Social Care Assessments**: Integration of housing, occupation, lifestyle, and support network information enables holistic needs identification and appropriate service provision. ### Care Coordination Interventions WRIST's professional contacts and care team documentation enables effective multi-disciplinary team working: **Integrated Care Planning**: The care and support plan structure links assessed needs to specific interventions, with clear ownership and review mechanisms. This supports the NHS Comprehensive Model for Personalised Care. **Transition Management**: Admission and discharge documentation with structured handover information reduces risks during care transitions, supporting interventions to prevent readmission and ensure continuity. **Shared Decision Making**: Documentation of capacity, consent, and personal preferences enables meaningful patient involvement in care decisions, supporting choice and control. ### Preventive Interventions The standard's comprehensive risk documentation and health maintenance tracking enables proactive care: **Immunization Programs**: Complete vaccination records with contraindication and adverse event tracking support population health management and outbreak response. **Screening Programs**: Problem list maintenance and risk factor documentation enable targeted screening interventions based on individual risk profiles. **Health Promotion**: Lifestyle factor documentation including smoking, alcohol use, and physical activity enables brief interventions and referral to prevention services. ### Safety and Quality Interventions **Medication Safety**: Structured medication records with allergy checking, interaction detection, and discontinuation tracking reduce adverse drug events. **Clinical Decision Support**: Standardized data capture enables real-time alerts and reminders for evidence-based interventions at point of care. **Outcome Monitoring**: Regular assessment using standardized tools enables tracking of intervention effectiveness and care plan adjustment.
