## Scenario 1: Complex Care Coordination for Frail Elderly Patient Mrs. Margaret Wilson, 84 years old, lives alone and has recently been discharged from hospital following a fall resulting in a fractured hip. She has multiple comorbidities including type 2 diabetes, heart failure, mild cognitive impairment, and is at risk of social isolation. This scenario demonstrates how WRIST enables coordinated care across primary, secondary, community, and social care services. | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Clinical Context** | Post-operative hip fracture, multiple long-term conditions, cognitive decline, high falls risk | | **Social Context** | Living alone, daughter lives 50 miles away, struggles with shopping and cleaning | | **Key Challenges** | Medication management, preventing readmission, maintaining independence, coordinating multiple services | | **WRIST Sections Used** | Problem List, Medications, Individual Requirements, Risks, Care Plan, Social Context, Professional Contacts | **Personas Involved:** P005 (Hospital Consultant), P008 (Discharge Coordinator), P001 (GP), P012 (District Nurse), P010 (Social Worker), P011 (Occupational Therapist), P014 (Informal Carer - daughter) **Business Processes:** BP001 (Comprehensive Assessment), BP003 (Care Planning), BP004 (Risk Assessment), BP007 (Discharge Planning) **Scenario Flow:** The Hospital Consultant updates the Problem List with "Fractured neck of femur" and reviews existing conditions. The Discharge Coordinator performs a comprehensive assessment, identifying high readmission risk due to falls history, polypharmacy, and limited social support. She documents Individual Requirements including mobility restrictions and cognitive support needs. The Occupational Therapist conducts a home assessment via WRIST, recommending grab rails and commode provision in Equipment and Adaptations. The Social Worker arranges a care package for daily visits, documenting in Services and Care. The GP receives automatic notification of discharge, reviews the updated medication list including new analgesics and bone protection, and schedules follow-up. The District Nurse accesses wound care instructions in the Care Plan and begins twice-weekly visits. Mrs. Wilson's daughter gains delegated access to view the care schedule and key contacts. ## Scenario 2: Mental Health Crisis Prevention David Chen, 28 years old, has bipolar disorder and has experienced three psychiatric admissions in the past two years. His Community Psychiatric Nurse (CPN) is working to prevent future crises through proactive monitoring and early intervention. This scenario shows how WRIST supports mental health care in community settings. | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Clinical Context** | Bipolar disorder type 1, previous psychotic episodes, good insight when well | | **Risk Factors** | Medication non-compliance, work stress, sleep disruption, cannabis use | | **Protective Factors** | Supportive partner, engaged with services, employed as software developer | | **WRIST Sections Used** | Risks, Contingency Plans, Assessments, Medications, About Me, Alerts | **Personas Involved:** P009 (Community Psychiatric Nurse), P001 (GP), P013 (Patient), P005 (Consultant Psychiatrist) **Business Processes:** BP004 (Risk Assessment), BP005 (Medication Management), BP008 (Crisis Planning) **Scenario Flow:** The CPN conducts fortnightly reviews using structured mood assessments, documenting scores in Assessments with trend monitoring. David self-reports early warning signs via the patient portal, updating his "About Me" section with triggers he's identified. When assessment scores indicate increasing mania risk, the CPN activates the pre-agreed crisis plan documented in Contingency Plans. This includes increasing visit frequency, liaising with the Consultant Psychiatrist for medication review, and alerting David's partner. The GP is automatically notified of the risk status change through the Alerts system. He wishes to die at home with family support. This scenario demonstrates how WRIST enables person-centered end of life care. | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Clinical Context** | Metastatic pancreatic adenocarcinoma, declining performance status, increasing pain | | **Personal Wishes** | Die at home, family involvement, Hindu religious needs, no intensive interventions | | **Family Context** | Supportive wife and three adult children, culturally specific end of life practices | | **WRIST Sections Used** | End of Life Care, About Me, Care Plan, Medications, Equipment, Professional Contacts | **Personas Involved:** P001 (GP), P012 (District Nurse), P005 (Palliative Care Consultant), P014 (Wife as Informal Carer) **Business Processes:** BP011 (End of Life Planning), BP005 (Medication Management), BP012 (Equipment Provision) **Scenario Flow:** The Palliative Care Consultant documents prognosis discussion and Robert's preferences in End of Life Care section, including DNACPR decision and preferred place of death. The "About Me" section captures religious and cultural preferences. Anticipatory medications are prescribed with clear guidance for symptom control. The GP coordinates the Gold Standards Framework approach, ensuring 24/7 access to palliative drugs. District Nurses provide daily visits with structured symptom assessment. Equipment including hospital bed and syringe driver is arranged through integrated procurement. Wife receives carer's assessment with respite options documented. She has incomplete immunization records and requires catch-up vaccinations according to UK schedule. This scenario shows pediatric prevention using WRIST. | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Clinical Context** | Incomplete immunization history, potential exposure to vaccine-preventable diseases | | **Social Context** | Refugee family, language barriers, temporary accommodation, trauma history | | **Public Health Priority** | Achieve age-appropriate immunization status, prevent disease transmission | | **WRIST Sections Used** | Vaccinations, Individual Requirements, About Me, Alerts, Appointments | **Personas Involved:** P002 (Practice Nurse), P001 (GP), Health Visitor, Interpreter **Business Processes:** BP013 (Immunization Management), BP001 (Comprehensive Assessment) **Scenario Flow:** The Practice Nurse conducts initial assessment with interpreter support, documenting communication needs in Individual Requirements. Available immunization records are entered into Vaccinations section with source verification. The GP reviews serology results to determine immunity status. A catch-up schedule is created following UK Health Security Agency guidance, with appointments automatically generated. Each vaccination administration captures full details including batch numbers and site. The system generates automatic reminders for subsequent doses. Alerts flag the incomplete immunization status to all healthcare contacts. The Health Visitor provides family support, monitoring developmental milestones alongside immunization compliance. By 6 months, Aisha achieves age-appropriate immunization status with complete documentation for school entry.
