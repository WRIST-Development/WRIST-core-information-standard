## Overview These user scenarios demonstrate how the Core Information Standard supports real-world care delivery across different settings and situations. Each scenario illustrates the data elements, workflows, and decision points involved in common care interactions. ## Scenario 1: Emergency Admission with Complex Needs ### Narrative Mrs. Dorothy Thompson, 78, is brought to the Emergency Department by paramedics after a fall at home. She lives alone, has mild dementia, diabetes, and heart failure. Her daughter Jane, who lives 50 miles away, is her primary carer but wasn't present during the fall. Mrs. Thompson appears confused and cannot reliably report her medications or medical history. ### Data Requirements | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Immediate Needs** | NHS number verification, allergy status, current medications, advance directives, resuscitation status | | **Clinical Data** | Problem list (dementia, diabetes, heart failure), recent observations, fall history, cognitive baseline | | **Social Context** | Lives alone, daughter as primary carer, home environment risks, support services in place | | **Communication** | Cognitive impairment affecting communication, daughter's contact details as key informant | | **Safeguarding** | Vulnerability due to dementia, potential self-neglect, need for capacity assessment | ### Personas Involved - **P014 - Paramedic**: Provides handover including circumstances of fall and initial observations - **P004 - ED Clinician**: Conducts assessment, reviews comprehensive record, makes admission decision - **P012 - Informal Carer (Jane)**: Provides collateral history via phone, confirms medication list - **P009 - Mental Health Practitioner**: Assesses capacity and delirium risk ### Business Process - Paramedic handover with scene safety assessment - Triage nurse accesses Core Information record - ED clinician reviews allergies, medications, and advance care planning - Contact with daughter to verify information and understand baseline - Capacity assessment documented - Admission decision with safeguarding referral ## Scenario 2: Planned Transition from Hospital to Care Home ### Narrative Mr. George Williams, 85, is ready for discharge after hip replacement surgery. He can no longer manage at home and has agreed to move to a care home. He has Parkinson's disease, requires assistance with daily activities, and takes multiple medications with specific timing requirements. His son Robert is actively involved in his care. ### Data Requirements | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Discharge Planning** | Functional abilities, mobility status, equipment needs, medication regime | | **Care Requirements** | Assistance with washing/dressing, medication prompts, dietary requirements, fall risk | | **Medical Management** | Parkinson's medication timing critical, post-operative care instructions, follow-up appointments | | **Legal/Consent** | Capacity for accommodation decision, son has LPA for property/finances | | **Preferences** | Daily routines, food preferences, social activities enjoyed | ### Personas Involved - **P003 - Hospital Consultant**: Oversees medical stability and discharge readiness - **P008 - Physiotherapist**: Assesses mobility and equipment needs - **P008 - Occupational Therapist**: Evaluates functional abilities and care needs - **P006 - Social Worker**: Coordinates care home placement and funding - **P010 - Care Home Manager**: Reviews requirements to ensure appropriate placement - **P007 - Care Coordinator**: Ensures seamless transition and information transfer ### Business Process - Multidisciplinary team meeting to plan discharge - Comprehensive assessment documentation - Care home assessment visit - Creation of detailed care transfer summary - Medication reconciliation with timing instructions - Handover meeting with care home staff ## Scenario 3: Chronic Disease Management in Primary Care ### Narrative Mohan Patel, 52, attends his GP practice for annual diabetes review. He works full-time as a bus driver, struggles with diet control, and has recently started experiencing numbness in his feet. His wife usually accompanies him to appointments to help with translation as English is his second language. ### Data Requirements | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Clinical Monitoring** | HbA1c trends, blood pressure, weight, foot examination findings, retinal screening status | | **Lifestyle Factors** | Shift work affecting meal times, limited exercise due to job, cultural dietary preferences | | **Risk Assessment** | Cardiovascular risk score, diabetic complications screening, occupational health implications | | **Communication Needs** | Prefers information in Gujarati, wife assists with translation, needs written materials | | **Care Planning** | Self-management goals, medication optimization, referral for diabetic foot assessment | ### Personas Involved - **P002 - Practice Nurse**: Conducts diabetic review and health education - **P001 - GP**: Reviews medications and makes referral decisions - **P011 - Patient (Mohan)**: Actively participates in goal setting - **P012 - Informal Carer (Wife)**: Supports communication and lifestyle changes - **P013 - Pharmacist**: Provides medication review and adherence support ### Business Process - Pre-appointment blood tests and data gathering - Structured diabetes review using template - Communication needs assessment and adjustment - Collaborative care planning with SMART goals - Referrals initiated for podiatry and dietetics - Follow-up arrangements documented ## Scenario 4: Mental Health Crisis with Safeguarding Concerns ### Narrative Sarah Jenkins, 35, is brought to A&E by police after a suicide attempt. She has a history of bipolar disorder, recent relationship breakdown, and her two children (ages 8 and 10) were present during the incident. She is known to mental health services but disengaged from treatment three months ago. ### Data Requirements | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Immediate Risk** | Suicide risk assessment, means used, protective factors, current mental state | | **Safeguarding** | Children witnessed attempt, assessment of parenting capacity, child protection considerations | | **Mental Health History** | Previous admissions, medication history, trigger factors, past risk behaviors | | **Social Situation** | Recent relationship breakdown, housing stability, support network, employment status | | **Legal Status** | Capacity for treatment decisions, need for Mental Health Act assessment | ### Personas Involved - **P004 - ED Clinician**: Initial medical assessment and stabilization - **P009 - Mental Health Practitioner**: Risk assessment and mental state examination - **P006 - Social Worker**: Child safeguarding assessment and family support - **P001 - GP**: Provides background information and medication history - **Police Officer**: Provides circumstances and scene information ### Business Process - Immediate medical assessment and treatment - Psychiatric assessment with risk formulation - Safeguarding referral for children - Capacity assessment for treatment decisions - Crisis plan development with safety planning - Coordination with children's services - Follow-up arrangements with crisis team ## Scenario 5: End-of-Life Care Planning ### Narrative John McCarthy, 72, has advanced lung cancer and wants to plan for his end-of-life care. He wishes to die at home, has specific views about life-sustaining treatments, and wants to ensure his wishes are known across all services. His partner David is supportive but struggling emotionally. ### Data Requirements | Category | Description | |---|---| | **Advance Care Planning** | Treatment preferences, DNACPR decision, preferred place of death, advance statement | | **Clinical Status** | Prognosis, symptom burden, current and anticipated care needs | | **Support Network** | Partner as main carer, carer assessment needs, family dynamics, bereavement support | | **Practical Arrangements** | Equipment needs, anticipatory medications, out-of-hours care plan | | **Coordination** | Hospice involvement, district nursing, GP support, specialist palliative care | ### Personas Involved - **P003 - Hospital Consultant**: Provides prognosis and treatment options - **P001 - GP**: Coordinates community care and provides ongoing support - **P005 - Community Nurse**: Manages symptoms and provides hands-on care - **Specialist Palliative Care Team**: Supports complex symptom management - **P011 - Patient (John)**: Actively involved in all planning decisions - **P012 - Partner (David)**: Requires support as carer ### Business Process - Advance care planning discussion initiated - Documented preferences across all settings - DNACPR form completed and shared - Electronic palliative care coordination system updated - Anticipatory medications prescribed and delivered - Regular review of preferences as condition changes - 24/7 care plan accessible to all providers ## Cross-Scenario Themes ### Information Criticality - **Time-critical scenarios**: Allergies, medications, advance directives must be immediately accessible - **Complex decisions**: Comprehensive social and clinical data supports holistic assessment - **Care transitions**: Complete, accurate information prevents errors and repetition - **Person-centered care**: Preferences and "About Me" information maintains dignity and individuality ### Data Quality Impact - Missing allergy information → potential adverse drug events - Incomplete social context → inappropriate discharge planning - Absent advance directives → unwanted interventions - Poor medication records → therapeutic failures or toxicity ### Workflow Integration Successful scenarios require: - Rapid access to core information at point of care - Clear mechanisms for updating and sharing changes - Integration with existing clinical systems - Support for multi-professional collaboration - Patient and carer involvement in data collection
