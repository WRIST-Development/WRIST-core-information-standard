## Overview of WRIST User Personas The WRIST Core Information Standard serves a diverse ecosystem of health and social care professionals, patients, carers, and administrative staff. These personas represent archetypal users who interact with WRIST-compliant systems in various capacities. Understanding their needs, competencies, and workflows is essential for successful implementation. ## Persona Summary Table | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key calculation, visualization | ## Detailed Persona Descriptions ### PER-001: General Practitioner (GP) The GP serves as the cornerstone of primary care, managing the full spectrum of health conditions across all ages. Within WRIST, GPs require rapid access to comprehensive patient information while managing high consultation volumes. They need to quickly review Problem Lists, current Medications, recent Investigation Results, and active Care Plans. GPs are heavy users of the clinical decision support features, relying on Alerts for drug interactions and Risk assessments for preventive care. They must efficiently document consultations in Contacts with Professionals while updating Problems, Prescriptions, and triggering Referrals. The 'About Me' section helps GPs deliver person-centered care during brief appointments. ### PER-002: Practice Nurse Practice Nurses deliver essential primary care services, particularly for chronic disease management and preventive care. They extensively use WRIST's Assessments section for structured reviews (asthma, diabetes, mental health), document Procedures and Therapies (wound care, vaccinations, health checks), and maintain Care and Support Plans. They need streamlined workflows for capturing Examination Findings and Investigation Results during clinic appointments. Practice Nurses often serve as care coordinators, updating Professional Contacts and ensuring Follow-up Appointments are scheduled. Their role in health promotion requires easy documentation of lifestyle factors in Social Context. ### PER-003: Hospital Consultant Consultants require sophisticated views of complex clinical data, often managing patients with multiple comorbidities. They need advanced filtering of Problem Lists by specialty, detailed Procedure histories with outcomes, and comprehensive Investigation Results with trending. Consultants heavily utilize the Formulation section for complex cases and lead multidisciplinary team discussions documented in Contacts with Professionals. They author detailed entries in Plan and Requested Actions, often with specific technical requirements. Access to Family History and previous Admission/Discharge Details is crucial for their decision-making. ### PER-004: Ward Nurse Ward Nurses require real-time access to actionable information during busy shifts. They need prominent display of Alerts, current Medications with administration schedules, and active Care Plans. They frequently document in Examination Findings (vital signs, fluid balance), administer medications requiring updates to the Medications section, and contribute to nursing assessments. Ward Nurses play a crucial role in discharge planning, updating the Discharge Details section and ensuring Contingency Plans are in place. They need mobile-friendly interfaces for bedside documentation. ### PER-005: Social Worker Social Workers navigate between health and social care systems, requiring comprehensive access to both domains. They extensively use Individual Requirements for understanding support needs, Legal Information for capacity and safeguarding, and Services and Care sections for coordinating packages. Social Workers conduct detailed Assessments using statutory frameworks and develop person-centered Care and Support Plans. They need robust Safeguarding documentation capabilities and must track Primary Support Reasons for reporting. Integration with local authority systems while maintaining WRIST compliance is essential. ### PER-011: Patient/Service User Patients increasingly expect digital access to their health information. They need user-friendly views of their Problem List, current Medications, upcoming Appointments, and Investigation Results in plain language. The 'About Me' section empowers them to share what matters most. Patients should be able to update Social Context information, communication preferences in Individual Requirements, and contribute to Care Plan goals. Privacy controls must allow them to manage access to sensitive information while supporting shared care. ### PER-013: Administrative Staff Administrative staff serve as the operational backbone, managing the flow of information and coordinating services. They need efficient interfaces for updating Demographics, managing Future Appointments, processing Referral Details, and handling Documents. While not making clinical decisions, they must understand enough clinical context to route information appropriately. They often serve as the first point of contact for queries about Investigation Results or Appointment scheduling. Training must emphasize data quality and the downstream impact of accurate administrative data. ## Competency Requirements Each persona requires specific competencies to effectively use WRIST: - **Technical Skills**: Basic computer literacy through to advanced data analysis - **Clinical Knowledge**: From general health awareness to specialist expertise - **Information Governance**: Understanding of confidentiality, consent, and data protection - **Communication**: Ability to translate between clinical and lay terminology - **Collaboration**: Skills for multi-professional and inter-agency working Implementation teams must design role-based access controls, user interfaces, and training programs tailored to each persona's needs and competencies.
