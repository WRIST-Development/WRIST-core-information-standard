## Overview The Core Information Standard enables a comprehensive range of health interventions by ensuring that essential patient information is available at every point of care. The standard supports evidence-based interventions across clinical, social, and person-centered domains. ## Key Clinical Recommendations ### Information Governance and Consent Healthcare providers must establish clear protocols for obtaining and documenting consent for information sharing. The standard includes specific data elements for recording consent decisions, including situations where individuals lack capacity or where legitimate representatives are involved. ### Reasonable Adjustments and Accessibility Under the Equality Act 2010, healthcare organizations have a legal duty to provide reasonable adjustments. The standard mandates systematic capture of: - Communication requirements (e.g., British Sign Language interpreters, easy-read materials) - Physical accessibility needs (e.g., wheelchair access, adjustable examination tables) - Sensory impairments requiring environmental modifications - Cognitive support requirements ## Intervention Categories | Intervention | Recommendation | of interventions based on: 1. **Patient safety impact** - Allergy documentation, medication reconciliation 2. **Regulatory requirements** - Safeguarding, mental capacity assessments 3. **Population health benefits** - Immunizations, screening programs 4. **Person-centered care** - Preferences, communication needs ### Quality Improvement The standard supports quality improvement initiatives by enabling: - Systematic data capture for audit and analysis - Identification of care gaps and unmet needs - Monitoring of intervention effectiveness - Benchmarking across organizations ### Integration with Care Pathways Interventions should be integrated into existing care pathways: - Emergency admission pathways - Priority on allergies, medications, advance directives - Discharge planning - Focus on social circumstances, support networks, follow-up arrangements - Chronic disease management - Emphasis on care plans, self-management support - End-of-life care - Advance care planning, preferred place of care/death documentation
