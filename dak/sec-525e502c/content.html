## Overview The following personas represent key user groups who interact with WRIST-based digital health systems. Each persona reflects real-world roles and responsibilities within integrated health and social care delivery. These personas guide system design decisions and ensure the digital adaptation meets diverse user needs across the care continuum. ## Primary Care Personas | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---|---| | P001 | General Practitioner (GP) | Primary care physician providing first-contact care, managing chronic conditions, and coordinating specialist referrals | Yes | Clinical assessment, diagnosis, prescribing, care coordination, health promotion | | P002 | Practice Nurse | Registered nurse delivering primary care services including chronic disease management, immunizations, and health screening | Yes | Clinical procedures, health assessment, patient education, care planning | | P003 | Practice Manager | Administrative lead ensuring efficient practice operations and regulatory compliance | Yes | Resource management, quality assurance, data governance, team leadership | | P004 | Community Pharmacist | Medicines expert providing dispensing, medication reviews, and minor ailment services | Yes | Medication management, clinical screening, patient counseling, formulary knowledge | ### GP (P001) - Detailed Role Dr. Sarah Chen is a General Practitioner at an urban practice serving 12,000 patients. She conducts 30-40 consultations daily, ranging from acute presentations to complex chronic disease management. Her interaction with the WRIST system includes reviewing comprehensive patient records before consultations, updating problem lists, prescribing medications with allergy checking, ordering investigations, making referrals, and coordinating care plans. She particularly values the "About Me" section for understanding patient preferences and the integrated view of hospital letters and test results. Her workflow demands rapid access to key information with minimal clicks. ### Practice Nurse (P002) - Detailed Role James Williams is a Practice Nurse specializing in chronic disease management. He runs dedicated clinics for diabetes, COPD, and cardiovascular disease, conducting annual reviews and providing ongoing monitoring. His WRIST system use centers on recording structured clinical observations, updating care plans, administering vaccinations with full documentation, and identifying patients requiring escalation to GP review. He relies heavily on assessment templates and decision support to ensure comprehensive reviews meeting Quality Outcomes Framework requirements. ## Secondary Care Personas | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---|---| | P005 | Hospital Consultant | Senior specialist physician managing complex cases in hospital settings | Yes | Specialist diagnostics, complex treatment planning, MDT leadership, teaching | | P006 | Junior Doctor | Trainee physician rotating through specialties, delivering frontline hospital care | Yes | Clinical assessment, basic procedures, handover communication, learning | | P007 | Ward Nurse | Registered nurse providing 24/7 inpatient care and treatment administration | Yes | Clinical monitoring, medication administration, care coordination, patient advocacy | | P008 | Discharge Coordinator | Specialist role ensuring safe, timely hospital discharge with appropriate follow-up | Yes | Discharge planning, risk assessment, service coordination, communication | ### Hospital Consultant (P005) - Detailed Role Mr. David Okonkwo is a Consultant Cardiologist leading a specialist heart failure service. She reviews inpatient episodes, assesses discharge readiness, arranges community services, and ensures medication reconciliation. Her WRIST interactions include comprehensive discharge planning documentation, updating social circumstances, arranging equipment and adaptations, coordinating with social services, and ensuring GP practices receive timely discharge summaries. She particularly values the integrated view of both health and social care needs. ## Community and Mental Health Personas | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---|---| | P009 | Community Psychiatric Nurse | Specialist nurse providing mental health care in community settings | Yes | Mental health assessment, medication monitoring, crisis intervention, therapeutic relationships | | P010 | Social Worker | Professional addressing social needs and safeguarding vulnerable individuals | Yes | Needs assessment, safeguarding, care coordination, advocacy, statutory duties | | P011 | Occupational Therapist | Allied health professional optimizing function and independence | Yes | Functional assessment, equipment prescription, environmental adaptation, rehabilitation | | P012 | District Nurse | Community-based nurse providing home nursing care | Yes | Complex wound care, medication administration, end-of-life care, care coordination | ### Community Psychiatric Nurse (P009) - Detailed Role Paul Thompson manages a caseload of 40 individuals with severe mental illness in the community. He conducts home visits, monitors mental state and medication compliance, provides crisis intervention, and coordinates with wider teams. His WRIST usage includes updating risk assessments with specific triggers and management plans, documenting mental state examinations, recording medication administration including depot injections, and maintaining crisis plans. He values mobile access for point-of-care documentation during home visits. ### Social Worker (P010) - Detailed Role Fatima Al-Hassan specializes in adult safeguarding and complex case management. She conducts comprehensive assessments under the Care Act, investigates safeguarding concerns, and coordinates multi-agency responses. Her WRIST interactions focus on detailed social assessments, safeguarding documentation with clear timelines, capacity assessments for specific decisions, care and support planning with personal budgets, and multi-agency information sharing. She requires robust audit trails and the ability to restrict access to sensitive information. ## Patient and Carer Personas | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---|---| | P013 | Patient/Service User | Individual receiving health and social care services | Yes | Self-management, health literacy, engagement with services, shared decision-making | | P014 | Informal Carer | Family member or friend providing unpaid care and support | Yes | Personal care, advocacy, care coordination, emotional support | | P015 | Care Coordinator | Professional organizing integrated care for complex patients | Yes | Case management, service navigation, advocacy, multi-agency coordination | ### Patient/Service User (P013) - Detailed Role Eleanor Roberts is a 72-year-old with multiple long-term conditions including diabetes, heart failure, and mild cognitive impairment. She accesses her WRIST patient portal to view test results, upcoming appointments, and care plans. She values being able to update her "About Me" section with preferences, record home monitoring results, and message her care team. Clear, accessible interfaces with adjustable text size are essential for her engagement. ### Informal Carer (P014) - Detailed Role Michael Roberts cares for his mother Eleanor, managing her medications, attending appointments, and coordinating services. Through delegated access to the WRIST system, he reviews appointment schedules, updates emergency contact information, logs concerns for clinical review, and accesses care plans to understand expected actions. He requires clear information about his mother's conditions and practical guidance on providing care. ## Administrative and Support Personas | Persona ID | Occupational Title | Description | Targeted | Key Competencies | |---|---|---|---|---| | P016 | Medical Secretary | Administrative professional managing clinical correspondence and appointments | No | Document management, appointment scheduling, correspondence processing, confidentiality | | P017 | Clinical Coder | Specialist extracting and coding clinical information for reporting and reimbursement | No | Clinical terminology, coding standards, data quality, audit processes | | P018 | Information Analyst | Professional analyzing health data for quality improvement and planning | No | Data analysis, report generation, quality metrics, visualization | These supporting personas interact with WRIST data for operational purposes but are not primary clinical users targeted by this DAK.
