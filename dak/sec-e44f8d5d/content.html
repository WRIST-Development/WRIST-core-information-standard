## Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) guides implementation of the **Core Information Standard v2.0** - the UK's foundational health information model for person-centered care records. Developed by the Professional Record Standards Body (PRSB), this standard defines essential data elements that enable seamless information exchange across NHS and social care settings, covering demographics, clinical data, social care needs, and patient preferences to support holistic care delivery. ## Background and Context ### Health System Context The UK health system faces increasing demands from an aging population, rising prevalence of chronic conditions, and the need for integrated care across multiple providers. The fragmentation of health records across primary care, secondary care, social services, and community providers has historically created barriers to effective care coordination. Patients often experience repetitive information gathering, delayed transfers of care, and safety risks due to incomplete information availability at the point of care. ### Policy Drivers Several key policy initiatives drive the adoption of standardized health information models: - **NHS Long Term Plan** - Emphasizes digital transformation and integrated care systems - **Data Saves Lives Strategy** - Promotes standardized data capture and sharing - **Integrated Care Systems (ICS)** - Requires seamless information flow across organizational boundaries - **Person-Centered Care Initiatives** - Mandate inclusion of patient preferences and social circumstances ### Clinical and Operational Challenges Healthcare professionals face daily challenges including: - Incomplete patient information during emergency admissions - Lack of visibility into social care arrangements and safeguarding concerns - Inconsistent medication reconciliation across care transitions - Limited access to patient preferences and advance care planning documentation - Fragmented risk assessments and care plans ## Relationship to Clinical Guidelines The Core Information Standard complements and enables the implementation of numerous clinical guidelines by providing the structured data foundation necessary for evidence-based care. It aligns with: - **NICE Guidelines** - Supports quality standards through consistent data capture - **WHO International Standards** - Compatible with international classification systems (ICD-11, ICF) - **SMART Guidelines** - Provides the data layer for implementing computable guidelines - **Specialty-Specific Standards** - Extensible to support domain-specific requirements ## Document Overview This DAK translates the Core Information Standard into actionable implementation guidance, including: - **Clinical recommendations** mapped to standard data elements - **User personas and workflows** demonstrating real-world usage - **Data specifications** with terminology bindings and validation rules - **Decision support logic** for key clinical scenarios - **Performance indicators** to measure implementation success - **Technical requirements** for system developers The following sections provide detailed guidance for each aspect of implementation, supporting both technical teams and clinical stakeholders in achieving successful deployment of standardized, person-centered health records.
