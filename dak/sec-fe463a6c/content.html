## Purpose and Scope This Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) provides comprehensive guidance for implementing the WRIST (Whole Record Information Standards Transfer) Core Information Standard v2.0.76 within digital health systems. The DAK serves as a bridge between the clinical information standard and its practical implementation in healthcare technology platforms, offering structured specifications that enable interoperable, person-centered health information systems. The WRIST standard represents a paradigm shift in health information management, moving beyond traditional episodic care documentation to encompass the complete health, care, and social context of individuals. This DAK translates the 47 concept groups and thousands of data elements into actionable technical specifications suitable for implementation in electronic health records (EHRs), care coordination platforms, and integrated care systems. ## Background and Health Domain Context The increasing complexity of healthcare delivery, particularly for individuals with multiple long-term conditions, mental health needs, and social care requirements, demands information systems that can capture and share comprehensive person-centered data. Traditional health records often fail to adequately represent the full context of a person's needs, fragmenting information across organizational and professional boundaries. ### Epidemiological Context In the UK, approximately 15 million people live with one or more long-term conditions, accounting for 50% of all GP appointments and 70% of hospital bed days. The intersection of health and social care needs affects millions more, with an estimated 1.5 million people receiving social care services annually. Mental health conditions affect 1 in 4 people, often co-occurring with physical health conditions and social vulnerabilities. This complexity necessitates information systems that can: - Capture holistic health and wellbeing information - Support care coordination across multiple providers - Enable person-centered care planning - Facilitate safeguarding and risk management - Maintain legal and regulatory compliance ### Policy Context The WRIST standard aligns with key UK health and care policies including: - **NHS Long Term Plan**: Emphasizing integrated care and digital transformation - **Data Saves Lives**: Promoting standardized, interoperable health data - **Integration and Innovation White Paper**: Mandating joined-up care delivery - **Care Act 2014**: Requiring person-centered assessment and care planning - **Mental Capacity Act 2005**: Ensuring appropriate consent and capacity assessment ## Relationship to Clinical Guidelines and SMART Guidelines This DAK operationalizes clinical best practices embedded within the WRIST standard, drawing from: - **NICE Guidelines**: Incorporating evidence-based assessment tools and care pathways - **Royal College Standards**: Reflecting professional body recommendations for documentation - **WHO SMART Guidelines**: Adopting the actionable, measurable approach to guideline implementation - **UK Core FHIR Profiles**: Ensuring technical interoperability with national infrastructure The WRIST standard's comprehensive approach supports implementation of multiple condition-specific guidelines within a unified information framework, avoiding the fragmentation that occurs when disease-specific systems operate in isolation. ## Document Overview This DAK contains ten sections providing complete implementation guidance: 1. **Health Interventions & Recommendations**: Core clinical actions derived from the WRIST standard 2. **Generic Personas**: Key user roles interacting with WRIST-based systems 3. **User Scenarios**: Real-world workflows demonstrating system usage 4. **Business Processes & Workflows**: Detailed process specifications for core activities 5. **Core Data Elements**: Complete data dictionary with clinical terminology mappings 6. **Decision-Support Logic**: Clinical rules and algorithms for automated support 7. **Indicators & Performance Metrics**: Quality and outcome measures 8. **Functional & Non-Functional Requirements**: System capability specifications 9. **References**: Supporting documentation and standards Each section provides actionable specifications ready for implementation by clinical system developers, integration teams, and healthcare organizations deploying person-centered information systems.
